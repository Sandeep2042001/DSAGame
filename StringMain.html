String
Defination:-
A "string" data structure represents a sequence of characters, such as letters, numbers, or symbols. It's commonly used to store and manipulate text in computer programs. Strings can be of fixed or variable length and offer operations like concatenation, comparison, and substring extraction. They are often treated as immutable in many programming languages, meaning they can't be modified once created, but instead, new strings are generated when changes are needed. Strings are fundamental for text processing in software development.
